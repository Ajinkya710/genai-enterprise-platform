# Claims Policy Overview

This document provides a high-level overview of claims policies and principles that guide claim handling activities.

## Policy Eligibility
Claims are only eligible for assessment if the policy was active at the time of the incident. Lapsed, cancelled, or expired policies are not eligible for coverage.

## Covered Events
Covered events are defined within the policy schedule and product disclosure statement. Examples may include accidental damage, theft, fire, or injury, depending on the policy type.

## Exclusions
Common exclusions include intentional damage, fraudulent claims, incidents occurring outside covered regions, and losses resulting from illegal activities. Exclusions must be clearly communicated to the claimant.

## Claim Timeframes
Claims must be lodged within the timeframe specified in the policy. Late claims may be declined unless exceptional circumstances apply.

## Claimant Responsibilities
Claimants are responsible for providing accurate information, cooperating with investigations, and supplying requested documentation in a timely manner.

## Insurer Responsibilities
The insurer is responsible for assessing claims fairly, transparently, and in accordance with applicable laws, regulations, and internal guidelines.

## Fraud Prevention
The insurer reserves the right to investigate suspected fraudulent claims. This may include requesting additional documentation or referring the claim to a specialist fraud team.
